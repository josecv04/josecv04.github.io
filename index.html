What I work on

I’m interested in building systems at the intersection of biology and electronics: front-end signal acquisition, biosensing, embedded firmware, and analysis pipelines that help interpret messy real-world data.

Recently, I’ve focused on modular EEG hardware, hydrogel-based active electrodes, and electrochemical sensing platforms designed for multi-biomarker workflows.

What I’m aiming for

My long-term goal is to contribute to medical devices and translational bioengineering research—especially where robust instrumentation and thoughtful system design make experiments and care more reliable.
